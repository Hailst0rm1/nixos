# Opportunity signals — the scorecard

Score every candidate on all five. Each score is 1–5 and **must cite a reason with a live URL**; a score with no evidence behind it is not a score.

| Signal | Question it answers | 1 (weak) | 5 (strong) |
|--------|--------------------|----------|-----------|
| **Pain** | How acute and how loud is the hurt? | Mild annoyance, few voices | Visceral, repeated, many independent complaints |
| **Market ceiling** | How much revenue is realistically on the table? | Tiny or hobby-scale | Large, funded, actively-spending buyers |
| **Underserved-ness** | How badly do current solutions fail? | Crowded, solved well | Whitespace, or incumbents widely hated |
| **Feasibility** | Effort to a demand-testable MVP? | Needs deep tech / regulation / capital | A landing page + fake door tests it |
| **Speed-to-signal** | How fast could you validate demand? | Long sales cycles, slow signal | Fast, self-serve, cheap to probe |

## Profile tag

After scoring, give each idea a one-line **profile tag** that names its shape — the honest summary a founder would say out loud. Examples:

- `starving niche, low ceiling` — high Pain + high Underserved, low Market
- `huge market, crowded, slow` — high Market, low Underserved, low Speed
- `underserved sweet spot` — high across Pain, Market, Underserved
- `quick MVP, modest ceiling` — high Feasibility + Speed, mid Market

## Merit hard, diversity soft

- **Merit is the hard filter.** An idea ships only if it is genuinely promising on the evidence. No filler, ever.
- **Diversity is a soft constraint.** Cap how many shipped ideas share the same profile tag (rule of thumb: no more than ~3 of one shape) so the slate is not monotone. This surfaces the natural range — the starving-niche play, the big-but-hard swing, the quick-MVP play — **only when the evidence actually contains them**. Never invent a contrarian idea to fill a shape.
