Is there a mattpocock/skills for business?

Whether a passionate, expert open-source "skills" repository for business functions — marketing, sales prospecting, GTM — exists for AI agents the way mattpocock/skills does for engineering. A five-lens synthesis: practitioner, academic, skeptic, economist, and historian. Every claim independently checked against its primary source before publication.

01

The 60-Second Summary

Yes — the thing you asked for now exists. As of 2026, real, passionately-authored business-skills repos ship for Claude Code: coreyhaines31/marketingskills (~36.8K GitHub stars, by a genuine marketing operator) covers CRO, copy, SEO and analytics; gtmagents/gtm-agents and sales-skills/sales cover prospecting, pipeline and enrichment; and Anthropic's own Claude for Small Business (May 13 2026) bundles marketing, lead-triage and margin workflows. But the mattpocock analogy quietly smuggles in a compiler. Matt Pocock's skills are trusted because the TypeScript type-checker adjudicates them — code either compiles or goes red. Marketing and sales output has no such oracle, so a business skill is one operator's taste frozen at write-time, decaying with the next fad. All five lenses converge on this: the form (a beloved expert's repo) is here; the substrate that makes it trustworthy is not. And the single highest-value ask — "find companies that would buy my product" — can't live in a markdown file at all: it needs live enrichment data that Clay ($3.1B) and Apollo ($150M ARR) sell behind a paywall.

03

The Hidden Connection

The Practitioner says "yes, it exists now"; the other four lenses say "it structurally can't exist." On the surface that's a flat contradiction — and it's the sharpest disagreement in the panel. But they're describing two different objects.

What exists is the form: a folder of SKILL.md files written by a passionate, credible operator. What can't yet exist is the substrate: a test oracle that makes each skill's quality checkable and lets trust compound across contributors. mattpocock/skills is trusted because the TypeScript compiler adjudicates every skill in it. coreyhaines31/marketingskills is popular because Corey is trusted — but nothing adjudicates the skills themselves. Both domains got the form. Only code got the oracle.

You already have the business-skills repo you asked for. What you don't have — and can't buy — is the compiler that tells you which skills in it are any good. For business output, you are the compiler.

The assumption this briefing rests on (and the missing 6th lens)

All five lenses froze the same variable: outcome. None measured whether a skill-generated campaign, cold email, or positioning statement actually performs better than the founder's own work or a plain prompt. The whole "no oracle" thesis assumes you can't build one — but that's untested, not proven.

The missing 6th lens is the Empiricist / end-user who A/B-tests skill output against a control on real conversion data. If skills measurably lift outcomes, you can bootstrap an oracle from those results (CRMArena-style), and the "business skills can never be verified" objection weakens sharply. If they don't, the repos are well-crafted scaffolding theater. This single omission could invert Findings 2, 3 and 5 — build with a way to measure, not just generate.

04

What To Actually Do Differently

For a founder/operator using AI agents who wants business-side skills today — concrete moves, not principles.

01
Install the repos that already exist — they're your scaffolds.

Start with coreyhaines31/marketingskills for marketing/CRO/copy, and gtmagents/gtm-agents or sales-skills/sales for GTM/prospecting. Add via the Claude Code plugin marketplace or npx skills add. This directly answers "does an equivalent exist" — it does (Finding 1).

02
For "find companies that would buy," wire a data tool — don't expect a skill to do it.

Connect Clay or Apollo via MCP and let the skill orchestrate the enrichment; the paid data source supplies the actual leads. A markdown file can't hold a lead list (Finding 4).

03
Be the taste layer — budget to rewrite ~40% of any marketing skill.

Treat every business skill as a strong first draft tuned to a generic ICP, never turnkey. Fork it to your brand voice and market. This is the human oracle the skill can't be (Finding 3).

04
Build your own mini-oracle from your best past work.

Keep a small eval set — your highest-converting past emails, your sharpest positioning — and grade skill output against it before shipping. That's the closest thing to a "compiler" for business output, and it's the 6th-lens move most people skip.

05
Prefer one opinionated brain over the mega-dumps.

A single credible operator's repo (Haines) beats a 200+-skill catalog of unmaintained slop. Density of judgment > breadth of coverage when nothing adjudicates quality (Finding 3).

06
Watch the vendor bundles as the maintained option.

Anthropic's Claude for Small Business (May 2026) is the early form of the "vendor bundles verified skills into a paid product" pattern history predicts (Finding 5). If you want maintenance over passion, that's where it'll land.

05

The Frontier Question

Will someone build a "CRMArena for GTM outcomes" — an oracle that scores a marketing or sales skill by real conversion lift — and if so, does the trusted repo become the one with the best eval scores rather than the most famous author?

This is the hinge. The entire panel agrees business skills can't compound trust because they can't be falsified. But that's a claim about today's tooling, not a law of nature. The moment an outcome-grounded benchmark exists for GTM output, business skills gain the same self-improving, merit-accruing dynamic that made coding skills explode — and authority shifts from reputation to measured performance. No lens addressed whether this oracle is buildable, because building it requires the one thing none of them had: your real conversion data. Whoever closes that loop first owns the category.
